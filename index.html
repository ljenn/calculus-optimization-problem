Swimming for the Reefs

In an island far far away, there laid a beautiful ocean spread across the horizons. From an underwater view, the island was layered with breathtaking coral reefs waiting to be discovered by a courageous explorer.


Actually, the island was more of the coast. It's somewhat far away since it's imaginary, though the ocean is still beautiful. And these reefs--they were already discovered.


Lining the coast underwater, these vibrant reefs are 60 meters deep and about 5 meters in height.


Parker, a female photographer, decides to capture a few snapshots of this exotic reef from faraway. She decides that snorkeling is the best method and wants to capture the largest possible view of the reefs--the best view.


However, there's an issue. Parker doesn't want to pay the snorkeling rental, which is a whopping $40 for one hour, when she only wants to stay out there for about 45 minutes. So she purchases $20 worth of swim goggles and a life vest from a shabby store down the street.


Unfortunately, this causes a couple more issues:

  1. Parker has to swim to her desired spot and back to the coastline in one hour because a security boat makes its rounds every hour.
  2. Parker is a terrible athlete; she can't swim. She can only doggy paddle and that will take her approximately 10 minutes every 50 meters. And she has to rest for 1 minute every 50 meters.


Nevertheless, Parker is unperturbed. She gears up and jumps in from one of the more deserted areas on the coastline.


What to Solve For

  1. At what distance, will she receive the largest possible view of the reefs?
  2. Side Question: Is one hour enough for her at this rate? Will she get away with the cheaper route or will she get caught and get into trouble? Should she just pay the $40 instead of suspiciously doing things her own way?
